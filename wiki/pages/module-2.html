Metlock Module 2

sfGFP Calibration and 96-Well Expression Tracker

Module 2 is the calibration and comparability layer of Metlock. In plain English, it helps the team learn how strongly the shared control system turns on before switching to functional proteins that may be harder on the cells.

The module uses sfGFP together with an Anderson promoter ladder and an AHL dose series to map induction conditions onto predictable expression strengths. That gives Metlock a common measurement language before the platform moves into higher-stakes functional outputs.

Description

Why Metlock calibrates expression before switching proteins.

Module 2 exists because a platform is only useful if its outputs can be compared safely and consistently. Instead of moving directly from promoter logic to functional zinc-handling proteins, Metlock first uses sfGFP as a readable proxy for expression level.

The technical idea is to combine an AHL dose series with an Anderson promoter ladder and a 96-well tracking workflow. That allows the team to ask where the inducible system behaves like a weak, medium, or strong constitutive promoter and to identify expression windows that are predictable enough to reuse across experiments.
