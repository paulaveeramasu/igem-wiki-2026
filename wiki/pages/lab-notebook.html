Lab Notebook

Keeping the wet-lab record practical and fillable.

The Lab Notebook page should act as a clean running record of experimental work. It needs to be organized enough for future team members to follow, but simple enough that new entries can be added without reworking the page structure each time.

This version creates a practical notebook format for timeline notes, protocols, controls, and future weekly entries.

Notebook Overview

What this page should help the team do.

A useful notebook page should document what was done, why it was done, what conditions were used, and what the outcome meant for the next step. It should not only serve as a memory aid, but also as a readable trail for teammates and future reviewers.

In practice, this means recording transformations, assay setup, controls, troubleshooting, and any adjustments to planned workflows in a way that stays easy to scan later.

Experimental Timeline

A place to track major milestones.

  1. Preparation Record stock preparation, DNA handling, media setup, and other pre-experimental steps.
  2. Transformation and build work Note chassis, DNA input, antibiotic selection, incubation conditions, and any immediate outcomes.
  3. Verification and controls Track which checks were planned, which were completed, and where results did or did not support moving forward.
  4. Assays and interpretation Add assay-day notes, measurement setup, and short statements of what was learned from the run.

Wet Lab Workflow

What the notebook should capture during experiments.

This section can hold concise wet-lab workflow notes: transformation conditions, outgrowth timing, antibiotic concentrations, plate setup, induction conditions, or assay-specific preparation details. The point is not to rewrite every protocol from scratch, but to record what was actually used in this project.

When experiments change, the notebook should document the difference clearly so later results make sense in context.

Controls and Verification

What must be tracked carefully.

Controls, verification steps, and troubleshooting checkpoints should be recorded clearly because they determine whether a result is interpretable. Positive controls, selection conditions, colony verification, and follow-up decisions should all have a traceable place in the notebook.

Data Recording Expectations

How to keep future entries useful.

Each entry should make it easy to answer a few basic questions later: what condition was tested, what changed from the last run, what control was included, what was observed, and what the result implied for the next experiment.

That level of consistency will make later Results and Engineering pages easier to write because the key experimental decisions will already be documented clearly.

Next Entries to Add

How the notebook can grow cleanly.

The next additions should include concise dated entries for transformations, verification attempts, assay setup, and any future calibration or binding experiments. The page is meant to stay practical, so short consistent entries are better than large unstructured blocks of text.
